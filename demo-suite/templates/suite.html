Introduction

We are going to use a common client-onboarding journey to showcase Enki’s features. You will need to complete an initial account setup process, and then connect the new client’s data to Enki and use Enki to share the data with another institution.

Pro tip since you'll be creating three accounts in the following process and you'll need to remember your username and password for all three it helps to keep things simple. You don't need strong passwords and all the details for all three can be the same.

Setup: create a customer of Bank A

First create a user who has a bank account but no relationship with Enki

  1. Go to Bank A and follow links to Open a bank account.
  2. Complete the registration process without doing any integration with Enki.
    Please note the only required fields are username and password (you'll need to remember these), and also first name, last name, email address and nationality.
  3. Make sure you can log into Bank A.

Your sample customer, let's call her Alice, is ready now for use in the demo. We'll follow her as she links her Bank A account with Enki, and then opens an account with another bank (perhaps she's investing or taking out a mortgage). We'll see how Enki speeds up the process and provides assurance that personal information is being handled securely and responsibly.

Customer demo script

    Step 1: Alice links her account with Enki

  1. Visit the Bank A profile page, sign in first, if necessary.
  2. Click Connect and then view confirmation message and note that the connect button has been disabled.

    3. Step 2: Alice opens an account with Enki and connects to Bank A

  3. Alice registers with Enki.
  4. Alice logs in and arrives at the Enki homepage. She clicks Link with bank-a.
  5. Alice is taken to Bank A and prompted to sign in.
  6. There she gives permission for Enki to view her data sharing agreements with Bank A and is then directed back to the Enki homepage where the button for linking to Bank A is now disabled.
  7. Alice visits the Enki dashboard tab after selecting Bank A she can see a summary of the personal information that it holds for her.

    8. Step 3: Alice users her Enki account while opening an account in Bank B

  8. Alice goes to Bank B and follows links to Open a bank account
  9. In the section first section of the form: In partnership with Enki she answers Yes
  10. Alice confirms that she gives permission to Enki share her personal information with Bank B. She can choose to select personal information she wants to import from which other PII holders (at this stage in the Demo that will only mean Bank A)
  11. Alice is returned to the Bank B sign-up process which now is now displaying the imported personal information, she completes the Bank B registration process.
  12. When prompted to share her account with Enki she clicks Yes, the transfer is confirmed and she complete sign up.
  13. Verify Alice can log into Bank B.
  14. Visit the Bank B profile. The button to connect the account with Enki should be disabled.
  15. Alice now returns to Enki homepage. She clicks Link with bank-b.
  16. Alice is taken to Bank B and prompted to sign in.
  17. She consents to share personal information held by Bank B with Enki and is redirected to Enki homepage.
  18. Alice returns to the Enki dashboard tab after selecting Bank B she can see a summary of the personal information that it holds for her.
